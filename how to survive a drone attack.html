HOW TO SURVIVE A DRONE ATTACK

1. If You’re in an Open Area

  • Seek Cover Immediately: Hide in thick concrete structures, underground spaces, or dense tree cover.
  • Stay Out of Sight: Drones use infrared and motion sensors. Avoid open fields and rooftops.

2. If You’re in an Urban Area

  • Blend In: Wear layered clothing to break up heat signatures.
  • Disrupt Signals: Use RF jammers or Wi-Fi interference to disable smaller drones.

3. If the Drone is Armed

  • Move Erratically: Avoid running in a straight line.
  • Watch for Hovering: A sudden stop may indicate an incoming attack.

4. Countermeasures

  • Lasers & Flashlights: Can interfere with drone cameras.
  • Net Guns & Slingshots: Useful for taking down small drones.

5. If a Strike is Imminent

  • Lie Flat: Feet facing the blast to reduce shrapnel exposure.
  • Open Mouth: Prevents internal damage from pressure waves.

6. Long-Term Precautions

  • Learn Drone Sounds: Recognize rotor noise for early detection.
  • Use Camouflage: Make tracking harder by blending with surroundings.
