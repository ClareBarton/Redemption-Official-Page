HOW TO DETECT AND AVOID LAND MINES

1. Recognizing High-Risk Areas

  • Common Locations: Former war zones, borders, abandoned roads, and bridges.
  • Warning Signs: Unusual soil, markers, dead animals, or destroyed vehicles.

2. How to Detect Land Mines

  • Visual Inspection: Look for unnatural mounds, wires, or metallic glints.
  • Use a Probe: A long, non-metallic stick can help detect mines safely.

3. Avoiding Land Mines

  • Stick to Known Paths: Follow marked trails and avoid fresh soil.
  • Move Carefully: Step lightly, distribute weight evenly, and avoid running.

4. If You Step on a Land Mine

  • Stay Still: Many mines are pressure-triggered and explode if you move.
  • Call for Help: Trained personnel can assist in deactivating the mine.

5. What to Do If Someone Else Steps on a Mine

  • Warn Others: Stop movement to avoid triggering more mines.
  • Mark the Danger Zone: Use objects to indicate a mined area.

Final Tip
